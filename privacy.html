How Privacy Pools Work

A technical overview of the protocol

1

Deposit & Proof Generation

Users deposit funds into the privacy pool and generate a zero-knowledge proof of their deposit.

Users select an association set - a subset of deposits they wish to be associated with, excluding known illicit deposits.

Technical Overview

The technology behind Privacy Pools

Zero-Knowledge SNARKs

Privacy Pools use zk-SNARKs (Zero-Knowledge Succinct Non-Interactive Arguments of Knowledge) to enable private transactions. Users can prove they made a valid deposit without revealing which deposit is theirs.

Association Set Proofs

Users generate proofs that their deposit is contained within their chosen association set. This allows them to exclude specific deposits (such as those linked to illicit activities) while maintaining privacy.

Merkle Trees

The protocol uses Merkle trees to efficiently represent the set of all deposits. Users prove membership in a subtree corresponding to their association set.

Smart Contracts

All logic is implemented in auditable smart contracts on the Casper Network, ensuring transparency and decentralization of the protocol.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Privacy Pools

How are Privacy Pools different from mixers?

Traditional mixers provide "all or nothing" privacy and can be sanctioned entirely. Privacy Pools allow users to dissociate from illicit deposits while maintaining privacy, reducing regulatory risk.

Is my privacy guaranteed?

Privacy Pools provide strong cryptographic privacy through zero-knowledge proofs. However, the level of anonymity depends on the size and diversity of your chosen association set.

What are association sets?

Association sets are subsets of deposits that users choose to associate with. By excluding known illicit deposits from your association set, you can maintain regulatory compliance.

Can I be forced to reveal my transactions?

You can optionally implement selective disclosure, which allows you to prove transaction details to specific parties (like regulators) without making the information public.

What are the fees?

Privacy Pools charge a small fee (typically 0.25-0.5%) to compensate liquidity providers, relayers, and provers who maintain the system.

How can I earn rewards?

You can earn rewards by providing liquidity, running relayers, or generating proofs for the Privacy Pool protocol. Visit the Dashboard to learn more.
